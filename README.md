# Closed-Loop DC-DC Boost Converter

Developed for **EE371: Advanced Electrical Engineering Laboratory** at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Guwahati, this project covers the full design cycle, simulation, and hardware implementation of a high-efficiency, closed-loop DC-DC Boost Converter. The system leverages an analog, op-amp-based Proportional-Integral (PI) controller to maintain a regulated $24\text{ V}$ output from a $12\text{ V}$ input at a rated power of $24\text{ W}$. Full hardware verification, LTSpice files (`CLBC.asc`), and schematic assets are documented within the repository.

## System Specifications & Theoretical Design
The power stage operates in Continuous Conduction Mode (CCM) at a switching frequency ($f_{sw}$) of $20\text{ kHz}$. Based on a target $12\text{ V}$ to $24\text{ V}$ step-up, the circuit runs at a $50\%$ duty cycle ($D = 0.5$) with a $24\ \Omega$ load resistance and an output current ($I_o$) of $1\text{ A}$. To restrict the inductor current ripple ($\Delta i_L$) to $\le 20\%$ ($0.4\text{ A}$), a $750\ \mu\text{H}$ inductor was designed and fabricated. This involved winding approximately 19 turns of 18 SWG copper wire around a Ferrite Ring Toroid Core. While theoretical math dictated a $10.42\ \mu\text{F}$ capacitor to hold the output voltage ripple ($\Delta V_o$) under $10\%$, a $47\ \mu\text{F}$ electrolytic capacitor was chosen in the final implementation to enhance transient stability and counter Equivalent Series Resistance (ESR).
## Control Architecture & Hardware Implementation
The closed-loop architecture feeds a scaled output voltage ($V_{scaled} \approx 0.415\text{ V}$, derived via a $91\text{ k}\Omega$ and $1.6\text{ k}\Omega$ potential divider) into a TL064 op-amp error amplifier. A parallel $5\text{ V}$ Zener diode provides over-voltage protection for the control loop. The resulting error signal is processed by the analog PI block and compared against a $20\text{ kHz}$ triangular carrier wave to dynamically adjust the PWM duty cycle. The physical layout was translated onto a two-layer PCB using Eagle, featuring wide high-current traces ($> 100\text{ mil}$), a single-point grounding strategy to isolate switching noise, and a MORNSUN isolated gate driver to safeguard the control ICs during operation.

## Experimental Results & Performance
Hardware validation using a Digital Storage Oscilloscope (DSO) confirmed a stable output voltage holding between $23.8\text{ V}$ and $24.1\text{ V}$ under full load, with slight drops attributed to the MUR1560 diode forward voltage and inductor ESR. The system demonstrated robust closed-loop performance; when the input voltage was varied between $10\text{ V}$ and $14\text{ V}$, the PI controller adjusted the PWM in real-time to keep the output within $\pm 2\%$ of the $24\text{ V}$ target. Transient load step-down overshoots were corrected by the integral loop within $15\text{ ms}$. Thermal management protocols—including mounting the IRF740 MOSFET and MUR1560 diode on aluminum heatsinks—successfully maintained operating temperatures below $60^\circ\text{C}$.
